The Great California Exodus

Why are people leaving California and where are they going?

By Max Cassett

What is happening in California?

California is known for Hollywood, In-and-out, the Golden Gate bridge. People move to the state for its beautiful weather and rich culture. You would be hard pressed to find any imperfection at a first glance at the Golden State, but let's look a little deeper. In 2023 it was reported that since 2018 millions have flocked from the California coast to other American states. Other studies including census done by the IRS have come to the same conclusion, people leaving the state droves. But why?

California has a problem with money. That's a broad statement but at its essence that's what it is. California has one of the most expensive housing markets, food prices, and transportation prices in the continental United states? Well why, one might say? Because of course it would, you would hear my shout from afar. It is the most populous state in the Union with dense yet large cities that have attracted people with its natural scenery and large job market. Throughout the last century people have moved to California en masse and now it's crowded and there is only so much to go around. The demand drives the price up massive percentage points which has created a huge divide between the upper, middle, and lower echelons of society. KTLA ( a Los Angeles news station) quoted “Michael A. Stoll, economist and professor in the Department of Public Policy at the University of California, Los Angeles” saying “As housing costs continue to rise, Americans are moving to lower-density, more affordable regions between expensive, economic-driving states.” This has created a problem in and of itself, made worse by politics.

People are often quick to label California as an inherently Blue state on the political spectrum but that my friend, is a generalization. There is a significant republican population in California exemplified by the last election for governor and president. Republicans are making moves in California no doubt but they are also literally moving. There is significant data presented by the IRS that indicates that Califonrians are moving to traditionally red states including Texas and Florida. Texas and Florida are considered more business friendly and have softer taxes and cheaper gas when compared to California, while keeping the warm weather and scenic views.

Another key driver of people away from California is the widespread homeless situation in her cities. California ranks number one in homelessness overall in the United States. In the cities the homeless are rampant and apparent, especially in the coastal cities of San Francisco, San Diego, and Los Angeles. The local governments have not allocated enough to local homeless shelters and policing to fix the situation so it is currently a situation that the residents have to live with.


The California government is in fact taking steps to mitigate the damage that's been done to its population numbers, but the question is begged, will it be enough? Reforms in homelessness and fixing the economy might go far for those who choose to bear it but time will only tell whether or not the population will reach its former numbers.
